Safety Guidelines

Your safety is our top priority. Follow these guidelines for a safe and enjoyable outdoor experience.

General Safety Guidelines

At CampWild, we want you to have an amazing outdoor experience while staying safe. Please review and follow these safety guidelines before and during your stay.

Before You Go

  • Research your destination thoroughly, including weather conditions, terrain, and potential hazards.
  • Share your itinerary with a trusted friend or family member who is not joining the trip.
  • Check that you have all necessary permits and reservations.
  • Pack appropriate clothing, gear, and emergency supplies.
  • Ensure your mobile phone is fully charged and consider bringing a portable charger.
  • Download offline maps of the area you'll be visiting.

Campsite Safety

Setting Up Camp

  • Choose a designated campsite or a durable surface to minimize environmental impact.
  • Avoid camping under dead trees or branches that could fall.
  • Keep a safe distance from cliff edges, water sources, and wildlife trails.
  • Store food properly in sealed containers or bear-proof storage when available.
  • Keep your campsite clean and organized to prevent trips and falls.

Fire Safety

  • Check if campfires are permitted in your camping area before starting one.
  • Use established fire rings or fire pans when available.
  • Keep fires small and manageable.
  • Never leave a fire unattended.
  • Keep a bucket of water and shovel nearby to extinguish the fire.
  • Fully extinguish your campfire before sleeping or leaving the campsite.
  • Ensure ashes are cool to the touch before departing.

Outdoor Activities Safety

Hiking and Trekking

  • Stay on marked trails and respect trail closures.
  • Hike with a partner or group whenever possible.
  • Carry a map, compass, or GPS device and know how to use them.
  • Pace yourself and take regular breaks, especially at high altitudes.
  • Carry sufficient water and snacks.
  • Wear appropriate footwear and clothing for the terrain and weather.
  • Be aware of changing weather conditions and have a plan to seek shelter if needed.

Water Activities

  • Always wear a properly fitted life jacket when boating, kayaking, or participating in water activities.
  • Never swim alone and be aware of your swimming abilities and limitations.
  • Check water conditions, including temperature, currents, and depth before entering.
  • Avoid alcohol consumption before or during water activities.
  • Keep a close eye on children near water at all times.
  • Be cautious of slippery rocks and uneven surfaces near water.

Wildlife Safety

Encountering Wildlife

  • Observe wildlife from a distance. Never approach, feed, or touch wild animals.
  • Store food, trash, and scented items securely to avoid attracting wildlife.
  • Make noise while hiking to avoid surprising animals.
  • Learn about the specific wildlife in your destination area and how to respond to encounters.
  • If you encounter a large predator, make yourself look big, make noise, and back away slowly.
  • Keep pets leashed and under control at all times.

Weather Safety

Weather Preparedness

  • Check weather forecasts before and during your trip.
  • Be prepared for sudden weather changes with appropriate clothing and gear.
  • Know the signs of hypothermia, heat exhaustion, and heat stroke.
  • Seek shelter immediately during thunderstorms and stay away from tall objects.
  • Apply sunscreen regularly, even on cloudy days.
  • Stay hydrated and drink water regularly, especially in hot weather.

Health and First Aid

Medical Preparedness

  • Carry a well-stocked first aid kit and know how to use its contents.
  • Bring any personal medications you might need.
  • Learn basic first aid skills before your trip.
  • Be aware of the nearest medical facilities to your camping location.
  • Consider carrying a whistle or other signaling device for emergencies.
  • Stay properly hydrated and nourished throughout your trip.

Emergency Contacts

  • Emergency Services: 911
  • CampWild Emergency Hotline: 1-800-555-9876
  • Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222
  • Park Ranger Station: Contact information provided in your booking confirmation

We recommend saving these numbers in your phone and writing them down on a card to keep in your wallet.

Remember, your safety is ultimately your responsibility. By following these guidelines and using common sense, you can help ensure a safe and enjoyable outdoor experience with CampWild.

If you have any questions about safety at a specific destination, please contact us before your trip.
